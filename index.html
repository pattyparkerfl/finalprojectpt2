Development of Periodic Table

In the early days of chemistry chemists largely worked alone and there was no universal or clear organization of elements. Scientists grouped the known elements either by atomic mass or by common properties. Dmitri Mendeleev felt that both atomic mass and propertes can be used to organize the elements. It is said he was inspired by the card game solitare in which the cards are arranged by suit and number. In 1869 Mendeleev published his periodic table of elements. The properties of the elements repeat in an orderly way from row to row of the table. This repeated pattern is an example of periodicity in the properties of elements. This is incredible as it allows you to know the properties of an element and how it will react with others based on its position in the periodic table. In fact, Mendeleev's table had many blank spots predicting the existence of elements that had not yet been discovered. We do not memorize the periodic table, we READ it.
